A little about me

I love designing and programming user experiences. I went into software development because I loved the field’s combination of problem solving and design.

After I graduated from Carnegie Mellon University with a degree in Technical Writing, I worked at an online publisher for 2 years as an editor.

I managed a staff of 26 people and answered to millions of readers. But I found that I spent more and more of my time fixing problems with the site than I did actually editing or writing articles; and I really enjoyed doing it! That exposure motivated me to make a career change to software development.

That’s why I went to App Academy. The program was extremely challenging and fast paced, but ultimately rewarding. I got to build great projects there like iTube, a YouTube inspired video sharing site, and askCarl, a web animations-teaching site.
